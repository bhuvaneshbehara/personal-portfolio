Modelling and simulation of fuel cell system for electric vehicle integration



Figure 1: Schematic Diagram of PEM fuel cell


Project objective:


         In this project, we analyse fuel cell voltage, current, and other crucial parameters for electric vehicle (EV) integration, this research explores modelling and simulation methodologies. System dynamics,proton exchange membrane activity, and electrochemical processes are precisely predicted by extensive computational modelling. By optimizing system performance, economy, and reliability under a range of operating situations, the study hopes to make it easier for fuel cell technology to be seamlessly integrated into the EV space. This research advances the viability and adoption of fuel cell powered electric vehicles and speeds up the development of sustainable transportation solutions by properly estimating voltage and current outputs together with other relevant properties.


Project Introduction:


         Fuel cell vehicles offer a sustainable alternative to fossil fuels, reducing greenhouse gas emissions and dependence on oil. Hydrogen is the most promising fuel, though infrastructure costs remain a challenge. Modeling and simulating fuel cell voltage, current, heat, and flow—using CFD, electrochemical, and system-level approaches—are essential for optimizing performance and enabling cleaner, more efficient electric vehicle technologies.

Modelling of Fuel Cell:



       Fuel cells are electrochemical conversion devices that produce electricity directly from the chemical energy of an oxidant and a fuel. The PEMFC's basic schematic diagram shown in fig.1. A fuel cell and a battery are similar in that they both have two electrodes and an electrolyte. Fuel cells are therefore one of the greatest substitutes for reducing the use of fossil fuels. There are several categories for fuel cells. Solid oxide fuel cells (SOFC), phosphoric acid fuel cells (PAFC), molten carbonate fuel cells (MCFC), and proton exchange membrane fuel cells (PEMFC) are more popular types of fuel cells.Because PEMFC operates at lower temperatures and has a higher power density than other types of FC systems, it is used in this work. PEMFC, the most well-known fuel cell, is therefore ideal for housing applications and provides a guarantee for medium power range applications. The electrochemical process of PEM fuel cells begins on the anode side, where flow plate channels bring in H2 molecules. Catalyst at the anode splits hydrogen into H+ and e-.Here, e-travels to the cathode via an external electrical circuit while H+ flows through membranes. At the cathode, heat and water H2O are produced when hydrogen protons H+ and electrons e-use a catalyst to mix with oxygen O2.

Results and Discussions:



      Simulation of fuel cell electric vehicles (FCEVs) offers vital new insights into their efficacy, performance, and feasibility as a mass market technology. Complex computer models enable researchers to investigate a variety of parameters, including fuel cell efficiency, range, and overall system dynamics. These models help improve FCEV design, identify difficulties, and develop plans to increase cost and dependability. They provide a methodology for analyzing the environmental effect of FCEVs, comparing them to battery-electric and combustion engine cars. Improving modeling and simulation approaches is crucial for accelerating the transition to fuel cell-powered, environmentally friendly transportation solutions as technology advances.
